BILLING AND INSURANCE INFORMATION GUIDE
Updated: November 2024
1. INSURANCE ACCEPTANCE
1.1 Accepted Insurance Plans
We are in-network with major insurance providers including:
- Medicare and Medicare Advantage plans
- Medicaid and Medicaid Managed Care
- Blue Cross Blue Shield (all plans)
- Aetna, Cigna, United Healthcare
- Humana, Kaiser Permanente (specific regions)
- Most PPO and HMO plans
1.2 Verification Process
- Insurance verified before elective procedures
- Verification takes 2-3 business days
- Patients notified of coverage details
- Prior authorization obtained when required
1.3 Out-of-Network Care
- Services may still be provided
- Higher out-of-pocket costs
- Balance billing may apply
- Payment arrangements available
2. PATIENT FINANCIAL RESPONSIBILITY
2.1 Co-Payments
- Due at time of service
- Amount determined by insurance plan
- Typically $25-$100 for specialist visits
- Emergency services: higher co-pays may apply
2.2 Deductibles
- Amount patient pays before insurance coverage begins
- Varies by insurance plan
- Applied to eligible services
- Resets annually (usually January 1)
2.3 Co-Insurance
- Percentage of costs shared with insurance
- Common: 80/20 or 70/30 split
- Applied after deductible met
- Subject to out-of-pocket maximum
2.4 Out-of-Pocket Maximum
- Maximum amount patient pays per year
- After reached, insurance covers 100%
- Varies by plan: typically $3,000-$8,000 individual
- Family maximum: typically $6,000-$16,000
3. BILLING PROCESS
3.1 Hospital Billing
- Initial bill sent within 30 days of discharge
- Itemized statement available upon request
- Electronic billing available
- Billing questions: (555) 123-4570
3.2 Physician Billing
- Separate from hospital charges
- Billed by individual physicians/groups
- May have different billing address
- Contact physician office for questions
3.3 Understanding Your Bill
- Hospital facility fees
- Professional fees (physicians)
- Laboratory and diagnostic tests
- Medications and supplies
- Operating room charges
- Anesthesia fees
4. PAYMENT OPTIONS
4.1 Payment Methods
- Credit/Debit cards (Visa, MasterCard, Discover, Amex)
- Cash or check
- Online payment portal: www.hospital.org/pay
- Phone payment: (555) 123-4570
- Mail payment: See address on statement
4.2 Payment Plans
- Interest-free payment plans available
- Minimum payment: $50/month
- Set up through Patient Financial Services
- Automatic payment option available
- No credit check required
4.3 Early Payment Discount
- 5% discount for full payment within 30 days

5. FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE
5.1 Charity Care Program
- For patients with financial hardship
- Based on household income and family size
- 100% discount available for eligible patients
- Partial discounts for those who qualify
- Application required (available at Admissions)
5.2 Eligibility
- Income at or below 400% of Federal Poverty Level
- Uninsured or underinsured patients
- Application reviewed within 30 days
- Documentation required: pay stubs, tax returns
5.3 How to Apply
- Request application at Admissions or online
- Submit completed application with documentation
- Financial counselor will review
- Notification within 30 days
6. UNINSURED/SELF-PAY PATIENTS
6.1 Self-Pay Discount
- 30% discount on all services
- Applied automatically
- No application required
- Payment arrangements available
6.2 Upfront Payment Options
- Pre-payment discounts available
- Estimate provided before elective procedures
- Payment plan available for emergency care
- Financial counseling recommended
7. MEDICARE AND MEDICAID
7.1 Medicare
- Part A: Hospital coverage
- Part B: Physician and outpatient services
- Medicare Advantage: All-in-one alternative
- Deductibles and co-insurance apply
- Medigap supplemental coverage accepted
7.2 Medicaid
- State-specific coverage
- Varies by state program
- Eligibility verification required
- Retroactive coverage may apply
- No balance billing for covered services
8. BILLING DISPUTES AND APPEALS
8.1 Disputing Charges
- Review bill carefully
- Contact Patient Financial Services within 60 days
- Provide documentation supporting dispute
- Response within 30 days
8.2 Insurance Claim Denials
- Hospital will file initial appeal
- Patient may need to file separate appeal
- Understand reason for denial
- Provide additional documentation if needed
8.3 Medical Billing Advocate
- Free assistance for billing questions
- Help understanding bills and insurance
- Available Monday-Friday, 9 AM - 5 PM
- Call (555) 123-4575
9. ADVANCE PAYMENT ESTIMATES
9.1 Pre-Service Estimates
- Available for elective procedures
- Contact Patient Financial Services 5 days in advance
- Based on insurance information provided
- Actual charges may vary
- Good faith estimate provided
9.2 Factors Affecting Costs
- Type of procedure
- Length of stay
- Complications or additional treatment
- Insurance coverage details
- Individual patient circumstances
10. ITEMIZED BILLING
10.1 Requesting Itemized Bills
- Free upon request
- Shows all charges in detail
- Includes dates of service and descriptions
- Available within 7 business days
- Request via phone, email, or patient portal
11. COLLECTION POLICIES
11.1 Payment Timeline
- Payment due within 30 days of billing
- After 60 days: may be sent to collections
- Multiple notices sent before collection
- Affects credit score if sent to collections
11.2 Avoiding Collections
- Communicate with Financial Services
- Set up payment plan before due date
- Apply for financial assistance
- Don't ignore bills or notices
