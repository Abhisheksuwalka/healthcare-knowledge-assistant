HOSPITAL ADMISSION POLICY AND PROCEDURES
Last Updated: November 2024
1. GENERAL ADMISSION INFORMATION
1.1 Admission Requirements
All patients seeking admission to our hospital must provide:
- Valid government-issued photo identification
- Insurance card or proof of financial arrangement
- Emergency contact information
- Current medication list
- Previous medical records (if applicable)
1.2 Admission Types
- Emergency Admission: 24/7 through Emergency Department
- Elective Admission: Scheduled in advance through physician referral
- Maternity Admission: Through OB/GYN department
1.3 Required Documents
- Consent for Treatment Form
- HIPAA Privacy Notice Acknowledgment
- Insurance Authorization Form
- Advanced Directives (if applicable)
2. PATIENT RIGHTS AND RESPONSIBILITIES
2.1 Patient Rights
Patients have the right to:
- Receive respectful and considerate care
- Know the names and roles of healthcare providers
- Refuse treatment (with informed consent)
- Privacy and confidentiality
- Access medical records
2.2 Patient Responsibilities
Patients are responsible for:
- Providing accurate health information
- Following prescribed treatment plans
- Respecting hospital staff and other patients
- Meeting financial obligations
- Following hospital policies
3. ADMISSION PROCESS
3.1 Pre-Admission (Elective)
- Physician referral received
- Insurance verification completed (2-3 business days)
- Pre-registration phone call
- Pre-admission testing scheduled if needed
3.2 Day of Admission
- Arrive 2 hours before scheduled procedure
- Check-in at Admissions desk (Ground Floor, Main Lobby)
- Complete registration and consent forms
- Financial counseling if needed
- Assigned to room/unit
3.3 Emergency Admission
- Triage assessment within 15 minutes
- Treatment begins immediately for critical cases
- Registration completed as soon as patient is stable
- Family notification
4. INSURANCE AND BILLING
4.1 Insurance Verification
- Must be verified before elective admission
- Co-pays collected at admission
- Out-of-network notifications provided
4.2 Financial Assistance
- Financial counseling available
- Payment plans can be arranged
- Charity care program for eligible patients
- Social services referral available
5. SPECIAL POPULATIONS
5.1 Pediatric Patients (Under 18)
- Parent/guardian must be present for admission
- Special consent requirements
- Family-centered care approach
- Child Life Services available
5.2 Geriatric Patients (65+)
- Comprehensive geriatric assessment
- Fall risk assessment
- Cognitive screening
- Discharge planning initiated at admission
5.3 Non-English Speaking Patients
- Professional interpreter services available 24/7
- Translated consent forms available
- Language assistance at no cost
